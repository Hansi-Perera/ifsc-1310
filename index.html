Web Accessibility Assignment

Web accessibility is the process of making your website accessible to as many people as possible, including people with impaired vision or hearing, cognitive or physical disabilities, and the elderly. Web accessibility also includes people with slower wifi, different devices, or browsers. There are many pros to making your website accessible. Most importantly, more people will access your website; with a greater reach, whatever information you are attempting to present with the site you've created will reach a larger audience. I think that is the most important from the developer's point of view, more people will see your thoughts and ideas. The second thing that comes to mind is the laws; you do not want to get flagged or face lawsuits that are not accessible to all people. It makes sense logically though, by not making your website easy to access, you may face allegations of discrimination even though that is not how you intended it to be. Lastly, it just seems more sustainable to have an accessible website. If your website is going to be around for a longer period of time, you want to make the necessary changes so that many groups and types of people will be able to access and continue to access the site you have created into the future.

Progressive Enhancement Assignment

Progressive Enhancement is a web development approach that starts with a solid foundation for web content and functionality, ensuring a basic experience for all users. Unlike the older approach of Graceful Degradation, which focuses on designing for the latest browsers, Progressive Enhancement prioritizes the core content and adds sophistication based on the user's browser or device capabilities. In this mindset, content is crucial, aiming to create a universally accessible web in line with Tim Berners-Lee's vision. The process begins by structuring content in rich, semantic HTML as the base layer. This content is then enhanced with a layer of CSS for presentation and JavaScript for interactivity. Progressive Enhancement is essential in modern web development for various reasons. It ensures inclusive accessibility, making content available to users with different technology levels, including those on older browsers, mobile devices, or with disabilities. By starting with a basic experience and progressively enhancing it, developers can cater to a broader audience while providing advanced features to users with modern browsers. Moreover, Progressive Enhancement offers future-proofing, allowing websites to adapt to new technologies and devices without compromising core content and functionality. It contributes to SEO friendliness, as well-structured semantic HTML makes content easily indexable by search engines. Additionally, separating content, style, and functionality makes the codebase more maintainable and adaptable to changing requirements. In summary, Progressive Enhancement aligns with the original ideals of the web by prioritizing content and accessibility. It provides a flexible and future-proof approach to web development, ensuring a positive user experience for a diverse audience.
